Privacy Policy

Last updated: 11 July 2026

FetchCast (“the app”) is a real-time weather, wind, and water dashboard for a curated set of lakes. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with your information.

The short version: FetchCast does not collect, transmit, or sell any personal information, and your location never leaves your device.

Information we collect

None. FetchCast has no user accounts, no sign-in, no analytics or crash-reporting SDKs, no advertising, and no third-party trackers. We — the developer — receive no data about you.

Location

FetchCast asks for “While Using the App” location access. Your location is used only on your device, for:

  • the man-overboard (MOB) marker — live bearing and distance back to a marked point;
  • showing your position on the radar and tracking maps;
  • the optional “Nearest Lake” mode, which picks the closest supported lake.

Your GPS position is never sent to us or to any third party. The weather, alert, forecast, and water-level requests the app makes are keyed to the fixed published coordinates of the selected lake’s dam, not to your location. Location access is never used in the background; you can decline it and still use the dashboard.

Network requests

To show live conditions, the app requests public data from these providers, using the selected lake’s fixed coordinates:

  • Open-Meteo (api.open-meteo.com) — weather, wind, and forecast
  • National Weather Service / NOAA (api.weather.gov, api.water.noaa.gov) — alerts, text forecasts, and lake/tailwater levels
  • Iowa State University / Iowa Environmental Mesonet (mesonet.agron.iastate.edu) — NEXRAD radar map tiles

As with any internet request, these providers can see the network metadata inherent to the connection (such as your IP address) and handle it under their own privacy policies. FetchCast sends them no account identifiers, no device identifiers, and no personal information.

Data stored on your device

The app stores the following locally, on your device only; none of it is transmitted to us:

  • the last successful conditions snapshot for each lake (so the dashboard works instantly and offline);
  • your lake selection and settings;
  • a man-overboard marker, if set, so it survives an app relaunch;
  • keys used to de-duplicate local alert notifications.

Notifications are generated on your device by iOS; there is no push server and no remote notification service.

Lakes and lake requests

FetchCast currently covers Kentucky Lake, Lake Barkley, Monroe Lake, Norris Lake, Douglas Lake, Cherokee Lake, and Watts Bar Lake. You may request additional lakes by emailing feedback@fetchcastapp.com; new lakes are prioritized based on user demand. A lake request contains only what you choose to put in your email, and we use it only to gauge interest and plan coverage.

Children

FetchCast is not directed to children and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13.

Safety notice

FetchCast is an informational aid, not a safety or navigation device. It relies on third-party data that may be delayed, incomplete, or inaccurate. Always verify conditions with official sources and use your own judgment. In an emergency, call 911 or use a VHF marine radio.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we will update the date above and post the revised version at the same URL: https://fetchcastapp.com/privacy.

Contact
